In the early 1960s, Streisand began her career by performing in nightclubs and Broadway theaters. Following her appearances on various television shows, Streisand signed with Columbia Records, insisting on full artistic control and accepting lower pay in exchange, an arrangement that she maintained throughout her career, and released her debut, The Barbra Streisand Album (1963), which won the Grammy Award for Album of the Year.
