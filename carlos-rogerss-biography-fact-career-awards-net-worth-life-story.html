Carlos Rogers is an American professional football player with an estimated net worth of $18 million.

Carlos Rogers started his career with the Butler High School’s football team. As a senior, he rushed for 1,332 yards, and 11 touchdowns. Also, he was named to the SuperPrep All-American, and Augusta Chronicle all-area.

He joined Auburn Tigers in college from 2001 to 2004. Rogers finished his career with 182 tackles, two fumbles recoveries, and seven inteceptions. He received the Jim Thorpe Award in 2004.

Rogers was chosen as the first round, 9th overall pick by the Washington Redskins during the 2005 NFL Draft. In 2009-2010 season, he appeared in 12 games, and recorded 12 pass break-ups, 54 combined tackles and two interceptions.

He left the Redskins and signed a one-year contract with the San Francisco 49ers in 2011. Rogers spent three seasons with the team and ranked 69th in the NFL Network’s Top 100 Players of 2012 list. He transferred to Oakland Raiders in 2014.

Carlos Cornelius Rogers was born on July 2, 1981 in Augusta, Georgia.
