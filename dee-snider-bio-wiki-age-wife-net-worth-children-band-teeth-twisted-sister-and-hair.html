Who Is Dee Snider?, Dee Snider Biography and Wiki

Dee Snider is an American singer-songwriter, screenwriter, radio personality, and actor who was born in Astoria, Queens, New York as Daniel “Dee” Snider.

Snider came to prominence in the early 1980s as lead singer as well as a songwriter of the heavy metal band Twisted Sister. Snider was ranked 83 in the Hit Parader’s Top 100 Metal Vocalists of All Time.

Dee Snider Twisted Sister, Tour, and Band

Snider joined Twisted Sister as well as became the sole songwriter of the band thereafter in early 1976. The group released their first studio album, Under the Blade, in September 1982 as well as developed a following in the UK. Less than a year later, Twisted Sister released their second album, You Can’t Stop Rock ‘n’ Roll.

Their third album, Stay Hungry, hit shelves on May 10, 1984. This became the band’s most successful record with the hits “We’re Not Gonna Take It” and “I Wanna Rock.” To emphasize the “twisted sister” image, Snider adopted a trademark persona of metal-inspired drag alongside blonde curly hair, an excessive amount of eye shadow and rouge, a Beauty mark as well as bright red lipstick.

1987’s Love Is for Suckers was the 5th Twisted Sister album. The record was first planned to be a Snider solo effort, but Atlantic Records encouraged a release under the Twisted Sister name. Touring lasted only into October that year and on the 12th of that month, Snider reported his departure from the band.

It was during this time that Snider formed Desperado which is a band featuring ex-Iron Maiden drummer Clive Burr, ex-Gillan guitarist Bernie Torme, and bassist Marc Russel. The group’s only album, Ace, has never been officially released but was heavily bootlegged on CD under the title Bloodied But Unbowed.

Snider formed Widowmaker alongside Joe Franco, a good friend to Twisted Sister as well as the drummer on the album Love Is for Suckers and Al Pitrelli and Marc Russel in the 1990s. The quartet recorded two albums with limited underground success, titled Blood and Bullets as well as Stand By For Pain.

Snider toured with a “self-tribute” band called Dee Snider’s SMFs (Sick Mother Fuckers), sometimes featuring ex-Twisted Sister drummer A.J. Pero in the late 1990s. The usual line up included Snider, Derek Tailer, Charlie Mills, Keith Alexander, as well as Spike.

Dee Snider House

Snider has a house in New York which is 6,000-square-foot East Setauket home.

The feature describes the decor of the home – which is a six-bedroom, 3.5-bathroom, three-level Victorian – as “a blend of marble, leopard print as well as ancient Roman influences.” It’s got two fireplaces, a second-story catwalk, and a man cave featuring air hockey, foosball, and retro video games. Dee Snider credited his wife Suzette with designing the home – and with finishing some of the details herself, such as staining cabinets, painting moldings, and sewing the curtains. “She’s really responsible for creating something beautiful, and people should see it,” Snider told New York Post.
