How Did Elizabeth Dorlene Sennett Die?

At 45 years old, Elizabeth Dorlene Sennett, affectionately known as Liz to her loved ones, was a compassionate member of the community and a dedicated family woman to her husband and two sons.

Being a grandmother brought her immense joy, highlighting her enthusiasm to embrace the journey she was on to the fullest. Consequently, the sudden and horrific loss of her life within the confines of her own Sheffield, Colbert County, residence sent shockwaves throughout the entire region, leaving its inhabitants deeply shaken.

On March 18, 1988, Reverend Charles Sennett arrived home from church as anticipated, only to be greeted by an unusual silence. To his dismay, he discovered his wife lying motionless in a pool of her own blood. Without hesitation, he promptly contacted the emergency services, but unfortunately, Elizabeth succumbed to her injuries shortly after at the nearby Helen Keller Hospital on South Montgomery Avenue. It became apparent that she had been subjected to a vicious assault, a fact confirmed by her autopsy which revealed that she had endured both brutal beatings with a galvanized pipe and multiple stab wounds to her chest, neck, face, and scalp.
