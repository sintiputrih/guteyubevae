How many times has Eminem Collabed with Nike?

Publish date: 2024-09-01

Eminem, born as Marshall Mathers, has been involved in numerous collaborations with Nike over the years. His partnership with the renowned sportswear brand has resulted in a collection of iconic sneaker releases that have not only left a significant mark in the sneaker industry but have also contributed to charitable efforts. Let's delve into the detailed analysis of Eminem's collaborations with Nike, exploring specific sneaker releases, design details, release dates, and unique features.

Eminem's Nike Sneaker Collaborations

Nike Artist Series 'Shady Burst' (2015)

In 2015, Eminem's collaboration with Nike resulted in the creation of the Nike Artist Series 'Shady Burst.' This sneaker featured a black Air Jordan 4 with a Carhartt twill upper, chrome accents, waxed laces, leather interior, and a translucent outsole. The design also incorporated an impression of Eminem's Jason Vorhees mask on one of the heel tabs, along with his reverse "E" logo on the other. This release not only showcased Eminem's unique style but also contributed to charitable efforts while celebrating the launch of his records.

Nike Air Force 1 Low 'Shady Records' (2004)

In 2004, Nike presented Eminem with several friends and family versions of the Air Force 1 Low to celebrate the release of his fourth major album, "Encore." The special pair featured a black, grey, and white color scheme, the Shady Records logo on the heel, and personalized Eminem details on the forefoot.

Nike Air Force 1 Low 'ENCORE'

The exact details of this collaboration are not provided in the search results. However, it's known that Eminem had a collaboration with Nike involving the Air Force 1 Low named "ENCORE".

Eminem x Carhartt x Nike SB Dunk (2023)

In 2023, Eminem teamed up with Carhartt and Nike to create a highly anticipated Nike SB Dunk. This sneaker, rumored to be limited to 50 pairs, is expected to feature a black upper with metallic silver accents and Eminem's iconic backward E logo in red, along with Carhartt branding. The proceeds from the limited release are expected to go to the Marshall Mathers Foundation, further emphasizing the charitable aspect of Eminem's collaborations with Nike.
