What are the three types of brainwashing?

Lifton ultimately defined a set of steps involved in the cases of brainwashing studied, roughly dividing them into three stages: breaking down the self, introducing the possibility of salvation, and rebuilding the self.

What makes someone brainwashed?

Someone is said to be brainwashed when their mind is controlled or altered by force, changing what they believe, think, value and do.

What are examples of brainwashing?

Brainwashing is commonly associated with cults and abuse. A cult leader might, for example, present a warm and friendly disposition to gain a person’s trust, then slowly break down the person by calling him or her names, forcing him or her to work, and engaging in other forms of abuse.

Who is susceptible to brainwashing?

Personality traits of brainwashing targets can determine how effective the process is. People who have a weak sense of identity and tend to experience guilt, great self doubt and black-and-white thinking are more likely to be successfully brainwashed.

What are the effects of brainwashing?

Brainwashing is said to reduce its subjects’ ability to think critically or independently, to allow the introduction of new, unwanted thoughts and ideas into their minds, as well as to change their attitudes, values and beliefs.
