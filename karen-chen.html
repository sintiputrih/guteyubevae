Karen Chen

Publish date: 2024-03-06

Karen Chen is a United States figure skater. Tammy Gambill trained her, and her routine was created by Drew Meekins and Pasquale Camerlengo.

Her total ISU score is 208.63, her short program score is 74.4, and her free skate score is 134.99. She was spotted competing in the 2022 Olympic Games in Beijing.

Where was Karen Chen born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Karen Chen was born on August 16, 1999 in Fremont, California, United States of America. Hsiu-Hui Tseng and Chih-Hsiu Chen are her parents’ names. She has a brother named Jeffrey Chen as well. Her parents immigrated to the United States from Taiwan in 1995, settling in Fremont, California.

Karen attended Connections Academy for homeschooling and graduated from high school. Her graduate studies were at Cornell University’s School of Human Ecology. She studies Human Biology, Health, and Society as a pre-med student at that university.

How did Karen Chen start her Professional Career?

Chen started her skating career after mastering the sport since 2005. In 2011, and again in 2012, she won gold at the National Intermediate level. She competed in the ISU Junior Grand Prix later in the 2013-14 season and qualified for the LPG Final. She also had type three tibia fractures in her right ankle.

In the 2014 World Junior Championships in Bulgaria, she finished seventh overall. In 2014-15, she won bronze at Czech Skate and silver at the Croatia Cup. She also competed in the 2015 World Figure Skating Championships, World Junior Figure Skating Championships, and US Championships.
