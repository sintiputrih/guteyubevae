Keenan Allen attended the University of California, Berkeley, and he played his college football for the California Golden Bears football team.

Furthermore, he was a backup wide receiver in his freshman collegiate football season.

However, after entering few matches as a sub, Allen’s amazing performance caught the eyes of the team coach, which earned him starting position in the team.

In fact, his mesmerizing performances in the 2010 season ended with 46 receptions for 490 yards and five touchdowns.

Moreover, the wide receiver Allen started to allure the supporter with his amazing performances during his second season with the Bears.

As a matter of fact, he completed his second college football with 98 catches for 1,343 yards, a receiving average of 13.7 yards, 6 touchdowns.

Meanwhile, in his third collegiate season, Allen continued his impressive run of form.

Nevertheless, the wide receiver Allen bagged 61 receptions for 737 yards, six touchdowns, and a rushing yard of 39 in the 2013 college football.

NFL career

With his prolific performance in college football earned, Keenan Allen earned the third-round draft with the 76th overall pick in the 2013 NFL Draft.

During his debut season in NFL, he was named the second wide receiver after the former Chargers’ wide receiver Antonio Gates.

Further, Keenan Allen signed a four-year contract with San Diego/ Los Angeles Chargers in May 2013

Besides, the entry-level deal was worth $2.81 million, which is quite impressive for a wide receiver role.

Subsequently, he made his NFL debut start against the Philadelphia Eagles and made six catches for 115 yards and a touchdown.

Furthermore, the Chargers wide receiver started to make an impact on the NFL after a game against Indianapolis Colts in week 14, after which he became the player with the highest reception (61) as a rookie player.

In addition, the wide receiver ended his first year of NFL with recorded 71 receptions, 1,046 receiving yards, and eight touchdowns.

Evidently, Allen’s mesmerizing rookie year performances earned him PFWA All-Rookie Team.

On top of that, he was named the PFWA Offensive player of the year as well as Pepsi NFL Rookie of the year in 2013.

Conversely, he ended his second NFL season with 783 receiving yards, four touchdowns, and career-high 77 receptions.

The following season was also disappointing for the Chargers wide receiver Allen, as he tore his anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) in game week 1.

Nevertheless, Allen came back strong in the 2017 NFL season as he earned himself his first-ever Pro Bowl for his 102 receptions for 1,393 yards and six touchdowns.

In fact, he was named three more Pro Bowl consecutively after getting his first in 2017.

Nonetheless, Keenan Allen has established himself as the prominent wide receiver for the Chargers, which has resulted in an $80.1 million four-year contract extension.

As of this writing, the Los Angeles Chargers wide receiver Allen has 624 receptions with 7,397 receiving yards, 54 touchdowns in total during his eight years of NFL career.
