Landon Donovan | Career

Club Career

He started his professional career playing for Bayer Leverkusen in 1999 but could not get enough playing time during the initial days.

Hence, he started his career playing for Bayer Leverkusen’s reserve team for the first two seasons. Later, he got promoted to the senior team.

Sadly, he could not bring out his best game for the senior team and was forced to loan to the MLS team San Jose Earthquakes.

Meanwhile, his loan spell with Earthquakes went well, where he managed to score 32 goals in 87 appearances.

Likewise, in 2005, Donovan signed a new team LA Galaxy in the MLS. Here, he produced his A-game and scored 112 goals in 247 appearances.

However, he has tried his luck in Europe for the second stint with German club Bayern Munich but failed to impress with his gameplay.

Further, he went to play for English side Everton on loan in 2010 but managed to score 2 goals in 10 appearances.

Surprisingly, he could not repeat the success story of MLS and returned to his own go-to league MLS with LA Galaxy in 2016 for his second stint.

Moreover, he played as a professional soccer player in MLS till 2019 and retired afterward.

International Career

He was an inspirational figure for his national team for excellent performance.

Meanwhile, he represented the national side for 157 matches and scored 57 goals, the highest number of goals scored for the US team.

Coaching Career

After retirement, Donovan is working as head coach for San Diego Loyal since 2020.
