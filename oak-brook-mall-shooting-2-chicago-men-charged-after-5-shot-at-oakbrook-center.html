Oak Brook mall shooting: 2 Chicago men charged after 5 shot at Oakbrook Center

Publish date: 2024-09-30
OAK BROOK, Ill. (WLS) — Two men from Chicago have been charged in last week’s shooting at Oakbrook Center Mall in west suburban Oak Brook.

The DuPage County State’s Attorney’s Office identified the suspects as 29-year-old Steve Lane and 32-year-old Tyran William.

Police said last Thursday, two people were exchanging gunfire with each other in a corridor area in the east part of the mall adjacent to the Nordstrom.

In addition to one of the suspects being shot, three woman and a man were wounded in the shooting. A sixth person, a 1oman, was injured while running away from the shooting.

Lane has been charged with one count of aggravated battery-discharge of a firearm and four counts of aggravated battery-discharge of a firearm toward a person and aggravated unlawful use of a weapon, prosecutors said.

Williams has been charged with five counts of aggravated discharge of a firearm and aggravated unlawful use of a weapon by a felon,prosecutors said.

Lane and Williams both appeared in bond court Wednesday, where judges set bond at $1 million for each of them.

Prosecutors said the shooting stemmed from a dispute over a verbal argument. They said a total of 12 shots were fired in the incident.

Between 15,000 to 20,000 people were at the mall at the time of the shooting, prosecutors said.

A third suspect remains at large, police said.
