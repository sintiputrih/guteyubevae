Rick Ross | Lifestyle

Despite all his riches, Ross’s ride has not been a pleasant one. At times he has had some trouble with the law.

He was arrested on marijuana and gun charges in January 2008.

Similarly, the Miami Beach cop arrested Ross, alleging his involvement with Triple C’s, a.k.a Carol City Cartel.

However, there was no proof of his affiliation with the gang.

Moreover, in June of 2010, author Freeway Ricky Ross filed a copyright infringement lawsuit in a California federal court against Rick Ross for using his name.

The court, however, ruled in favor of Rick Ross in December of 2013, allowing him to keep the name based on the First Amendment ruling.

Furthermore, Ross has never been married but has four children; William Roberts III, Berkeley Hermes Roberts, Billion Leonard Robert, and Toie Roberts.

He is a Christian and is pretty vocal about it. He prays every time he goes to the stage to perform.

On 27th January 2013, Ross and his girlfriend were the targets of a drive-by shooting in Fort Lauderdale, Florida.

Luckily both Ross and his girlfriend were unhurt but ended up crashing a car in an apartment building in an attempt to escape the shooting.

Rick Ross | Career

Before entering and making a name for himself in the music scene, Rick Ross worked as a correctional officer from December 1995 until he resigned in June 1997.

After quitting his job after working for 18 months, he debuted under the pseudonym Teflon Da Don.

However, he changed his name to Rick Ross in the mid-2000s, which was inspired by the former drug lord “Freeway” Rick Ross.

Moreover, he released his debut album in 2006 entitled ‘Hustlin‘.

Following the success of his debut album, he was offered by P. Diddy’s record label Bad Boy Entertainment and also from Irv Gotti’s Murder Inc.

Nonetheless, Ross signed a multi-million dollar deal with Jay-Z’s Def Jam Records.

He released another album entitled ‘Port of Miami‘ which became a smash hit debuting at number one on the Billboard 200 album chart.
