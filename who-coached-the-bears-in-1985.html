Who Coached the Bears in 1985?

Publish date: 2024-09-29

In 1985, the Chicago Bears were coached by two influential figures in the NFL, namely Mike Ditka and Buddy Ryan. Let's delve into the roles and impact of these coaches on the historic 1985 Chicago Bears season.

Mike Ditka: Head Coach of the Bears

Mike Ditka was the head coach of the Chicago Bears during the 1985 season. He led the team to an impressive 15–1 record, earning him the title of NFL Coach of the Year by the Associated Press. Under his leadership, the Bears achieved remarkable success, culminating in their dominant victory in Super Bowl XX. Ditka's coaching prowess was instrumental in guiding the team to their iconic season.

Contributions and Style

Ditka was known for being the offensive mind behind the 1985 Bears, and he played a pivotal role in shaping the team's playing style. He worked with key offensive players such as quarterback Jim McMahon, wide receiver Willie Gault, and the legendary running back Walter Payton. His strategic acumen and leadership were crucial in orchestrating the team's offensive success throughout the season.

Super Bowl Triumph

The pinnacle of Ditka's coaching career in 1985 was the Bears' resounding 46–10 victory over the New England Patriots in Super Bowl XX at the Louisiana Superdome in New Orleans. This historic win solidified Ditka's legacy as a remarkable coach who led the Bears to their first Super Bowl title.

Regrets and Legacy

Ditka has expressed regrets about certain decisions during the Super Bowl, particularly regarding Walter Payton's involvement in scoring touchdowns. However, his overall impact on the team and the sport is undeniable, as evidenced by the Bears' remarkable performance throughout the 1985 season.

Buddy Ryan: Defensive Coordinator

Buddy Ryan served as the defensive coordinator for the 1985 Chicago Bears. He was the mastermind behind the renowned "46" defense, which allowed an NFL-best 12.4 points per game and featured prominent defensive players such as Richard Dent, Dan Hampton, and Mike Singletary. Ryan's defensive expertise and innovative strategies were pivotal in shaping the Bears' formidable defense during the 1985 season.

Adversarial Relationship

It is worth noting that Ryan's relationship with head coach Mike Ditka was largely adversarial. Despite their professional collaboration, the two coaches had a strained relationship, with disagreements about coaching positions and strategies. This tension culminated in a notable incident during a late-season Monday-night game against the Miami Dolphins, where Ryan and Ditka engaged in a physical altercation, leading to their separation and an unsalvageable relationship.

Legacy and Departure

Despite the challenges in his relationship with Ditka, Ryan's impact on the Bears' defense was undeniable. His departure in 1986 to become the head coach of the Philadelphia Eagles marked the end of an era for the Bears, with his contributions leaving a lasting legacy on the team's defensive prowess.
